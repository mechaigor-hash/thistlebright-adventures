# Thistlebright Adventures — Example Campaigns

## Campaign 1: The Missing Moon-Muffins
**Length:** 3 short sessions.
**Premise:** Ailsa Thistledown baked moon-muffins for the fairy ceilidh, but they floated away.

### Session 1: Crumbs on the Fairy Path
- Wonder: A glowing path through bluebells.
- Problem: Moon-muffins are drifting into the sky.
- Scenes: Talk to a brownie, follow silver crumbs, cross polite stepping stones.
- Ending: Recover the first basket and find a feather clue.

### Session 2: Cloud Sheep Trouble
- Wonder: A hilltop where sheep float like clouds.
- Problem: Cloud sheep ate the moon-sugar by mistake.
- Scenes: Herd sheep with ribbons, calm a sneezy dragonling, collect moon-sugar.
- Ending: Dragonling offers warm cave oven.

### Session 3: Ceilidh Under the Rowan
- Wonder: Fairy dance under a guardian tree.
- Problem: Grumpy sproutlings blocked the party path.
- Scenes: Make fair rules, decorate lanterns, sing a welcome song.
- Ending: Feast, dance, title reward: Moon-Muffin Rescuer.

## Campaign 2: The Loch That Forgot Its Reflection
**Length:** 2–4 sessions.
**Premise:** The loch cannot reflect the moon, so night travelers are lost.

### Key scenes
1. Kelpies ask riddles in ripples.
2. Selkie Storykeeper reveals a stolen mirror-shell.
3. Castle Gargoyle Calf accidentally hid it while practicing guarding.
4. Heroes return it and the loch shines again.

## Campaign 3: The Tartan Door in the Hill
**Length:** one-shot, 30 minutes.
**Premise:** A tartan-patterned door appears in a grassy hill. Behind it, the fairy post office is upside down.

### Scene flow
- Knock politely or solve the plaid pattern puzzle.
- Catch flying letters by color.
- Help the post-mouse sort invitations.
- Decide who receives the mysterious golden envelope.

## Campaign 4: Dragon Sneezes at Castle Heatherhorn
**Length:** 1–2 sessions.
**Premise:** A tiny heather dragon sneezed and turned all castle signs backward.

### Encounters
- A maze of wrong signs.
- Gargoyle Calf teaches stone directions.
- Find peppery purple flowers causing sneezes.
- Make a calm-breathing song.

## Random Adventure Seeds
Roll 1d6:
1. A rainbow bridge lost the color green.
2. A brownie needs help before the royal fairy inspection.
3. A cloud sheep is stuck in a chimney.
4. A selkie library book is overdue by 100 years.
5. The Rowan Guardian wants a new bedtime story.
6. The fairy queen’s crown is actually a bird nest now.

## Ready-to-Run One-Shot: The Rowan Lantern Parade
**Length:** 35–45 minutes  
**Best for:** 2–5 young heroes  
**Big feeling:** Wonder, teamwork, giggles  
**Safety note:** Nothing chases to harm anyone. If a scene feels too spooky, say **Lantern** and the mist becomes friendly bubbles.

### Guide read-aloud
“Tonight is the Rowan Lantern Parade. Fairy folk, brownies, selkies, and gentle kelpies bring lanterns to the old rowan tree so every path in Thistlebright Glen glows safely. But oh no! The lantern lights have bounced away like golden peas. A worried fairy called Peigi Twinklecap flutters up to you. ‘We need brave, clever, kind helpers before the ceilidh music starts!’”

### What really happened?
A baby starling heard the ceilidh fiddles and tried to dance on the lantern rope. The rope twanged, and the lights popped loose. The lights are playful, not dangerous. They will return if the heroes make them feel welcome.

### Scene 1 — Heather Hill Clues
**Place:** A purple heather hill with tartan ribbons tied to little sticks.  
**Problem:** Three lantern lights are hiding in the heather and copying sheep noises.  
**Ask:** “How do you find a giggling light?”

**Checks:**
- **Kind 5+:** Speak gently; the lights hop into a jar with a happy hum.
- **Clever 5+:** Notice the “sheep” glow gold whenever they baa.
- **Quick 5+:** Catch one as it bounces, then invite the others to race back.
- **3–4 twist:** A light lands on someone’s nose and makes them sneeze sparkles.
- **1–2 complication:** The lights scatter into a funny shape; a friend may help with +1.

**Reward clue:** A tartan ribbon points toward the loch.

### Scene 2 — The Polite Kelpie at Mirror Loch
**Place:** Misty blue loch, stepping stones, and moon reflections.  
**Problem:** A friendly kelpie has lantern lights tangled in its mane but is too shy to ask for help.  
**Kelpie voice:** Musical, splashy, and polite: “If ye please, wee heroes, could you untwinkle my fringe?”

**Three solutions:**
1. **Kind:** Compliment the kelpie and gently comb the lights free with reeds.
2. **Clever:** Solve its ripple riddle: “What shines when shared?” Answer: “Light,” “kindness,” or any sweet idea.
3. **Brave/Quick:** Step stone to stone and toss a soft scarf for the lights to land on.

**Funny twist ideas:** wet socks that squeak, a frog applauds, the kelpie’s mane becomes a silly braid.

**Reward clue:** The freed lights drift toward the fairy ceilidh circle.

### Scene 3 — Ceilidh Circle Choice
**Place:** The old rowan tree, lantern hooks, tiny mushroom stools, and musicians tuning fiddles.  
**Problem:** The last lantern light refuses to hang up because it wants to dance too.  
**Choice:** Do the heroes make a rule, invent a dance, or design a special lantern job?

**Possible endings:**
- **Dance ending:** Everyone performs the “Thistle Wiggle”; the last light dances at the front of the parade.
- **Helper ending:** The light becomes the “First Lantern” and leads lost guests safely.
- **Craft ending:** The heroes decorate a lantern with tartan ribbon so it feels special.

### Finale read-aloud
“The rowan tree glows gold and green. The loch reflects every lantern, the brownies pass moon-muffins, and the kelpie stamps a splashy beat from the shore. Peigi Twinklecap bows. ‘By thistle, heather, loch, and star, you are Friends of the Rowan Light!’”

### Rewards
Each hero chooses one:
- **Title:** Friend of the Rowan Light.
- **Tiny treasure:** A paper lantern charm that glows when someone says please.
- **Map reward:** The safe glowing path to the Fairy Ceilidh Circle is now open.

### If you have 10 extra minutes
Ask each player to draw their lantern. Let every drawing add a tiny magical power: smells like biscuits, makes rainbows, hums lullabies, points home, or warms cold hands.

## Mini-Campaign: The Selkie Star Map
**Length:** 4 sessions of 25–35 minutes  
**Best for:** families or classrooms who want a gentle continuing story  
**Big feeling:** Mystery, friendship, ocean sparkle, high-fantasy wonder  
**Guide promise:** Nobody is trapped forever. The sea is magical but kind. Any wave, shadow, or loud sound can become bubbles when someone says **Lantern**.

### Campaign premise
Morag Shellsong, a selkie Storykeeper, keeps a star map that helps friendly night travelers find warm hearths, safe bridges, and the fairy ceilidh path. One windy evening the map breaks into four glowing star-stickers and flutters across Thistlebright Glen. The heroes collect the stars by helping creatures who each need kindness, cleverness, bravery, or quick feet.

### Campaign countdown track
Draw four empty stars on paper. At the end of each session, colour one in. When all four shine, the map becomes whole and points to a celebration.

| Star | Place | Who needs help? | Main choice | Reward |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Shell-Beach by Mirror Loch | Morag the selkie | Listen, sort shells, sing | **Listening Star** |
| 2 | Brownie Bakery Burrow | Brochan the brownie | Tidy, trade, invent | **Helpful Star** |
| 3 | Castle Heatherhorn Tower | Gargoyle Calf | Encourage, puzzle, climb | **Courage Star** |
| 4 | Rowan Ceilidh Circle | Fairy musicians | Share, dance, lead | **Welcome Star** |

### Session 1 — Shell-Beach by Mirror Loch
**Read aloud:** “Moonlight turns the loch silver. On the pebbly shore, a selkie in a soft seal-skin cloak pats her pockets. ‘Och, my star map has gone skittering! Without it, travelers may take the long soggy way home.’ Four tiny lights blink across the glen like shy fireflies.”

**Problem:** Shells are all mixed up, and the first star hides under the shell that sounds like home.

**Three ways to help:**
- **Kind 5+:** Ask Morag about her favourite home-sound. She smiles and says, “Waves, warm soup, and friends laughing.”
- **Clever 5+:** Sort shells by sound: whisper, hum, clack, and giggle.
- **Quick 5+:** Catch the rolling giggle-shell before it splashes into the loch.

**3–4 twist:** A shell sings your name in a very squeaky voice.  
**1–2 not yet:** The shell pile wobbles into a tiny tower. A friend can steady it with +1.  
**Reward:** The **Listening Star** sticks gently to the party map.

### Session 2 — Brownie Bakery Burrow
**Read aloud:** “The smell of oatcakes and moon-muffins drifts from a round green door. Inside, Brochan the brownie is floury from boots to eyebrows. ‘The Helpful Star landed in my dough, and now every muffin wants a job!’”

**Problem:** The muffins are marching around carrying spoons, napkins, and one very important star.

**Three ways to help:**
- **Kind 5+:** Thank each muffin for helping, then invite it to rest on a cooling rack.
- **Clever 5+:** Make a jobs chart: spoons in the pot, napkins on the table, star on the map.
- **Brave/Quick 5+:** Lead a tiny muffin parade safely around the oven rug.

**Funny complications:** flour moustaches, hiccuping raisins, a muffin that insists it is the captain.  
**Reward:** Brochan gives the **Helpful Star** and a basket of imaginary moon-muffins for sharing.

### Session 3 — Castle Heatherhorn Tower
**Read aloud:** “Castle Heatherhorn rises from the heather like a friendly old giant. At the top, a young gargoyle calf sniffles stone tears. ‘I was practising my guard pose and the Courage Star got stuck on the weather-vane!’”

**Problem:** The tower stairs are safe but very tall, and the gargoyle calf feels embarrassed.

**Three ways to help:**
- **Kind 5+:** Tell the gargoyle one thing they did well. Their wings perk up.
- **Clever 5+:** Notice the weather-vane turns when everyone says a rhyming direction: ‘Star so bright, turn just right!’
- **Brave/Quick 5+:** Climb the gentle stair, taking a wiggle break halfway.

**3–4 twist:** A tartan flag wraps around someone like a superhero cape.  
**1–2 not yet:** The stair creaks a silly tune; pause, breathe, and try a new rhythm.  
**Reward:** The **Courage Star** joins the map, and the gargoyle teaches a proud-but-polite guard pose.

### Session 4 — Rowan Ceilidh Circle
**Read aloud:** “At the rowan tree, fairy fiddles tune themselves and lanterns glow like honey. The final Welcome Star bounces above the dance floor, but it will not land until everyone has a place in the ceilidh.”

**Problem:** Three guests feel left out: a shy kelpie at the loch edge, a cloud sheep behind the tree, and a sproutling who only knows stompy dancing.

**Three ways to help:**
- **Kind 5+:** Invite each guest by name and ask what dance feels comfortable.
- **Clever 5+:** Design a dance with three parts: splash, fluff, stomp.
- **Quick 5+:** Lead a gentle follow-the-leader parade around the rowan.

**Final choice:** Where does the completed star map live?
1. With Morag, so stories guide travelers.
2. In the brownie bakery, so every visitor finds supper.
3. Under the rowan tree, so the ceilidh path always glows.

**Final read aloud:** “The four stars join with a soft chime. Lines of gold curl between loch, bakery, castle, and rowan tree. Every path home shines warmly. Morag bows, Brochan waves a floury spoon, the gargoyle calf stands tall, and the ceilidh begins. By thistle and star, the heroes are named Keepers of the Friendly Map!”

### Campaign rewards
Each hero chooses one keepsake:
- **Shell Compass:** hums softly near a safe path.
- **Brownie Button:** smells like biscuits when someone shares.
- **Gargoyle Pebble:** helps you stand tall for one brave sentence.
- **Ceilidh Ribbon:** adds sparkles to a dance or song.

### Printable recap boxes
Copy one box after each session so young players remember the story.
